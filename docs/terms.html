The Contract,
Written to Be Read.

Same principle as our pricing: if you have to hire someone to find out what you agreed to, it was written badly. Here are the terms, in the plainest English the law allows.

Last updated: 24 July 2026  ·  These terms govern use of the Delteris website and the supply of the Elenchon platform.

Plain summary. You subscribe monthly to an Elenchon appliance we install in your facility. You own your data and the hardware. We keep the software and the Phrakton fabric running, and credit you when the fabric misses its uptime target. There is no minimum term and no exit fee — if you leave, your historical records stay readable for audits.

The summary is for orientation only. Sections 1 to 19 are what actually applies.

2. Definitions

  • Appliance — the physical server installed at your site that runs the Elenchon software.
  • Platform — the Elenchon software, in the plan you have subscribed to.
  • Fabric — Phrakton, the zero-trust network overlay we operate to broker remote access to the Appliance.
  • Worker — an individual account able to log in and record work on the Platform.
  • Customer Data — everything you or your workers enter into or generate on the Appliance.

3. What we supply

We deliver, install and configure the Appliance at your facility, license you the Platform for the duration of your subscription, and operate the Fabric that carries remote access. The Platform executes entirely on the Appliance. There is no cloud instance of your deployment, and no functionality is withheld pending a network connection to us.

4. Plans, workers and add-ons

Current plans, included worker counts and add-on prices are published at delteris.com/pricing.html and form part of these terms.

  • Lite includes 20 Workers and may be expanded by a single block of ten, to a maximum of 30 Workers. Beyond that, an upgrade to Standard is required.
  • Standard includes 50 Workers and may be expanded by unlimited blocks of ten.
  • Each expansion block of ten Workers carries 20 GB of recurring remote bandwidth per month at no additional charge.
  • Additional remote bandwidth is sold in blocks of 100 GB, as a one-off top-up or a recurring monthly allowance.
  • Unused bandwidth does not roll over between billing periods.

4.1 Changing your plan

Upgrades, downgrades and add-on changes are applied remotely over the Fabric at no charge. No engineer visit is required and no commute fee arises. Upgrades take effect on request and are invoiced pro rata for the remainder of the current period. Downgrades take effect at the start of your next billing period, and we do not refund the current one. If a downgrade would leave you with more active Workers than the target plan allows, you must deactivate the excess before the change is applied.

5. Fees, invoicing and payment

Subscription fees are quoted per Appliance, per month, excluding VAT, and are invoiced monthly in advance. VAT is applied at the rate in force in Portugal, or reverse-charged where you supply a valid EU VAT identification number. Invoices are payable within 30 days of issue.

Overdue amounts accrue statutory commercial interest under Decreto-Lei n.º 62/2013. If an invoice remains unpaid 30 days after a written reminder, we may suspend remote access and support. We will not disable the Appliance or lock you out of your own data over a payment dispute.

We may revise published prices with 60 days' written notice. A price increase entitles you to terminate at the end of the notice period without penalty.

6. Deployment, training and travel

One-time deployment, training and travel charges are published on the pricing page and quoted before any work begins.

  • The Standard engagement includes a pre-deployment scoping visit, on-site installation, and a floor training programme that may be run as many times as your rollout needs.
  • The Lite deployment and training package is a single fixed price that covers installation and one training session. It does not include a pre-deployment scoping visit, and further training sessions are charged at the published rate plus travel.
  • Travel is charged per visit at the published commute rate.

Deployment dates are agreed in writing. Capacity is limited because our own engineers perform every installation; we schedule in the order that deposits are received.

7. Hardware

Hardware is supplied at our documented acquisition and delivery cost with no markup, and title passes to you on payment. It is yours: you may service it, relocate it or dispose of it. Manufacturer warranties pass through to you, and we make no separate hardware warranty of our own. You may also supply your own hardware, provided it meets the specification we publish for the deployment.

8. Uptime commitment

We commit to 99.9% monthly availability of the Fabric and issue invoice credits automatically when we miss it, on the scale published at delteris.com/pricing.html#uptime. No claim, ticket or argument is required. The commitment covers the Fabric — controller and edge routers — and is measured by our telemetry across each calendar month. It excludes your local internet uplink, pre-notified maintenance windows, and events outside our reasonable control. Because the Platform runs locally, an outage of the Fabric does not stop work on the shop floor.

These credits are your sole financial remedy for availability shortfalls.

12. Term, cancellation and what happens after

Subscriptions run month to month with no minimum term. Either party may cancel with 30 days' written notice, effective at the end of the following billing period. We may terminate immediately for material breach that remains uncured 30 days after written notice.

On termination your licence to use the full Platform ends, but the Appliance stays in your facility and your historical records remain readable in a permanent view-only mode, so you can satisfy audits without a live subscription. We will not remotely disable, wipe or repossess the Appliance. On request, and at the published rate, we will attend to export your data in a documented open format.
