Elenchon is an on-premise appliance, so the honest version of a privacy policy is a short one. Here is exactly what we process, why, for how long, and what you can demand of us.

Last updated: 24 July 2026  ·  Applies to delteris.com and to the commercial relationship between Delteris and its customers.

The short version. Elenchon runs entirely on hardware inside your facility. Your projects, weld records, qualifications and worker data stay on that appliance — we do not collect them, copy them, or hold them on any server of ours. This policy therefore covers a deliberately small amount of data: what you send us when you get in touch, and what is needed to run a contract.

1. Who we are

The controller of the personal data described in this policy is [[LEGAL ENTITY NAME]] ("Delteris", "we", "us"), a company registered in Portugal under number [[NIPC]], with its registered office at [[REGISTERED ADDRESS]].

You can reach us about anything in this policy at contact@delteris.com. We have not appointed a Data Protection Officer, as we are not required to.

2. What this policy covers

This policy explains how we handle personal data as a controller: visitors to this website, people who contact us, and the individuals who administer a customer account. It does not describe the data your own organisation stores inside its Elenchon appliance — for that data your organisation is the controller and we are not a recipient. Section 6 explains the narrow exception for remote support.

3. Personal data we process

3.1 Website visitors

This site is a set of static pages. It sets no cookies, runs no analytics, embeds no tracking pixels and has no advertising. We do not build profiles of visitors and we cannot identify you from a visit.

Our hosting provider (see section 7) automatically records standard technical request data, including IP address, timestamp, requested page and user-agent string, for the purposes of serving the site and protecting it from abuse. We do not access these logs for any other purpose.

The site loads typefaces from Google Fonts. Making that request discloses your IP address and browser details to Google. If you would prefer this not to happen, a browser extension that blocks third-party font requests will prevent it, and the site will render with system fonts.

3.2 People who contact us

When you email us we process the content of your message and whatever contact details you include — typically a name, an email address, a company and, if you volunteer it, a phone number. We use this to answer you and to keep a record of the conversation.

3.3 Customer account and billing contacts

To enter into and run a contract we process the name, business email, business phone number, role and company details of the people who represent a customer, along with the invoicing details required by Portuguese tax law.

3.4 Remote support and diagnostics

Where a customer has the Remote Access Module, our engineers may connect to the appliance over the Phrakton fabric when the customer asks us to. Sessions are initiated on request, and access is logged. In the course of diagnosing a fault an engineer may incidentally see operational data held on the appliance. We do not extract it, we do not retain copies, and we act solely on the customer's instructions — that is, as a processor. A data processing agreement is available on request and is signed as part of onboarding where required.

4. Why we process it, and on what legal basis

  • Answering enquiries — our legitimate interest in responding to people who contact us, or steps taken at your request prior to entering a contract (Art. 6(1)(f) and 6(1)(b) GDPR).
  • Performing the contract — deployment, support, billing and account administration (Art. 6(1)(b)).
  • Keeping accounting and tax records — compliance with a legal obligation under Portuguese law (Art. 6(1)(c)).
  • Site security and availability — our legitimate interest in keeping the site online and free of abuse (Art. 6(1)(f)).

We do not use personal data for automated decision-making or profiling, and we never sell it.

5. How long we keep it

  • Enquiries that do not lead to a contract: up to 24 months from the last message, then deleted.
  • Customer contact and contract records: for the duration of the contract and for 5 years afterwards, to cover the general limitation period.
  • Invoices and accounting records: 10 years, as required by Portuguese tax legislation.
  • Remote support session logs: 12 months.
  • Hosting request logs: retained by our host under its own retention schedule, typically a matter of days.

We keep the list of third parties deliberately short. We do not sell, rent or trade personal data, and we do not disclose it for advertising.

  • [[HOSTING PROVIDER — e.g. GitHub, Inc. (GitHub Pages)]] — serves this website.
  • [[EMAIL PROVIDER]] — hosts our mailboxes and therefore processes correspondence.
  • Google Ireland Limited — delivers the web fonts used on this site.
  • [[ACCOUNTANT / BILLING PROVIDER]] — issues and files invoices.

We may also disclose data where we are legally required to do so, or to establish or defend a legal claim.

7. Transfers outside the EEA

Our providers may process data outside the European Economic Area. Where that happens, the transfer is covered by the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses or an adequacy decision. Customer manufacturing data is never transferred anywhere: it stays on the appliance in your facility.

8. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to access your personal data, to have it corrected or erased, to restrict or object to our processing of it, and to receive it in a portable format. Where we rely on legitimate interests, you may object at any time, and we will stop unless we have compelling grounds not to.

Write to contact@delteris.com to exercise any of these. We will respond within one month. Exercising them is free, and we will not treat you differently for doing so.

If you believe we have handled your data badly, we would rather hear it from you first — but you have every right to complain directly to the Portuguese supervisory authority, the Comissão Nacional de Proteção de Dados (CNPD), at www.cnpd.pt.

9. How we protect it

The same engineering that we sell is the engineering we run on. Remote access to customer appliances is brokered through the Phrakton zero-trust fabric with post-quantum-safe encryption, mutual authentication and no listening ports exposed to the public internet. Internally, access to personal data is limited to the founders and to engineers who need it for a specific task.

10. Changes to this policy

If we change this policy we will update the date at the top of the page. Where a change materially affects customers, we will say so by email rather than expecting anyone to re-read the page.
